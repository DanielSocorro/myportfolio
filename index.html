My history

Hello, my name is Daniel Socorro, I’m Venezuelan living in Chile and yes, my name means help in Spanish so that's awesome because it's basically what I've done in my entire professional life.

I’m 38 years old and I graduated as a Systems Engineer 6 years ago.

Long before graduating, I was working in an administrative systems development company as an analyst and customer support; and although I loved to help, I was not happy with what I was doing, I wanted to code.

Then, when I finished in that company, I fulfilled my dream of being a front-end developer in a pharmaceutical company also in my country, but I could only work in web development for a year, since I had to forcefully leave my country due to the growing social, economic and political problems that the country was going through at that time.

I arrived here in Chile by land, crossing 4 different countries in Latin America: Colombia, Ecuador, Peru and finally Chile with only $ 500 dollars in my pocket.

After three weeks, I got a job again, in IT Support, and although I was doing a great job, helping many people in the company, I still couldn't fully enjoy it.

So I need to make a decision, and recently I decide to give a chance to my professional Career and it was not going to be too late for me to pursue my dream and become a developer, and radically change my life, so for that I quit my last job recently and I have been learning full time, for the past 4 months the latest programming technologies, working hard in my education and now I’m ready to apply my knowledge into practice to be the best developer I can become.

While I don’t have a big work experience as a front-end developer and it was a long time ago, I’ve had a lot of exposure to the technological business environment and, some of my courses involved working with real companies to solve real problems.
